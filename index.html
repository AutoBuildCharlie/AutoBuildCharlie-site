So... what's a workflow?

Great question. Think of a workflow like a recipe your computer follows — step by step — without you having to do anything.

💡

The Simple Version

A workflow is just a list of steps that happen automatically. Like: "When I get an email from a customer, read it, figure out what they need, and write a reply." That whole process = one workflow.

🎧

A Real-Life Example

Imagine every time someone fills out a contact form on your site, the AI automatically sends them a friendly reply, saves their info, and reminds you to follow up. You set it up once — it runs forever.

🤖

Where AI Comes In

Normal automation just follows rules. AI automation actually thinks. It can read messages, write responses, make decisions, and learn from your style — all on its own.

In short: A workflow is "If THIS happens → do THAT." With AutoBuildCharlie, you add AI into the middle so it can think, write, and decide — not just copy and paste.

How it works

3 steps to build your first AI automation

Seriously — you can have your first one running in under 10 minutes.

1

Pick your starting point

Choose what kicks off your automation. A new email? Someone filling out a form? A specific time of day? That starting event is called a trigger — it's what tells the AI "go!"

2

Tell the AI what to do

Add steps — like "read the message," "write a reply in my tone," or "save this to a spreadsheet." You write simple instructions. The AI figures out how to follow them.

3

Turn it on and walk away

Hit go. Your automation runs on its own — 24/7, even when you're asleep. You can check in anytime to see exactly what it did.
